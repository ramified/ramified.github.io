⋮⋮basic statistics▾
⋮⋮littlewood–richardson decomposition▾
Draw λ and μ, choose stable or fixed-rank mode, then compute.

Fixed-rank mode enumerates dominant candidate highest weights inside the invariant norm bound ‖γ‖ ≤ ‖λ‖ + ‖μ‖, then computes coefficients by Freudenthal characters plus Brauer–Klimyk straightening; the dimension check reports completeness.

⋮⋮lie algebra invariants▾
Open this card to compute invariants of Vλ at the weight μ.

Uses the selected Dynkin type/rank. The weight μ is interpreted by the same Young-diagram → Dynkin-label convention as the canvas heading.

⋮⋮2d weight slice▾
Draw λ and μ; this card shows their relative geometry as Lie algebra weights.

λ and μ must generate a 2-dimensional subspace of the weight lattice (i.e. they must be linearly independent) to enable the slice canvas.

⋮⋮kostka tableaux▾
Compute all semistandard Young tableaux of shape λ and weight μ. This card is independent of the selected Lie type.

Rows are weakly increasing and columns are strictly increasing; the weight μ = (μ₁, μ₂, …) means entry i appears μᵢ times.

⋮⋮kronecker decomposition▾
Compute all nonzero Kronecker coefficients gνλ,μ for |λ| = |μ|.

The output lists every nonzero target ν under the current browser cap; no third diagram is required.

⋮⋮plethysm decomposition▾
Compute all nonzero coefficients in the Schur plethysm sλ∘sμ.

The output lists every nonzero target ν under the current browser cap; no third diagram is required.

⋮⋮schur functor▾
Compute 𝕊μ(Vλ) for the selected Dynkin type/rank and decompose it as a sum of Vγ.

This card interprets λ as the highest weight of Vλ for the selected Lie algebra, applies the Schur functor with shape μ, then decomposes the resulting character into irreducible representations Vγ.

⋮⋮grassmannian cup product▾
Compute σλ ∪ σμ in H*(Gr(r,n)), where r is the number of grid rows and n−r is the number of grid columns.

The Young-diagram presentation uses Littlewood–Richardson coefficients inside the current r × (n−r) rectangle. The Chern presentation uses the Giambelli determinant in Chern classes ci.
